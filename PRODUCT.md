# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Serving is used by church ministry leaders, worship team admins, volunteers, and members of Estacao 337 congregations. Admin users manage members, teams, roles, schedules, and event details. Member users check invitations, accept or decline scale assignments, and review event notes or playlist links before serving.

## Product Purpose

Serving exists to make church scale management simpler, clearer, and more reliable without becoming a full SaaS product. It replaces a weak scheduling experience with a local-first operational tool for organizing worship, sound, projection, reception, and ministry participation. Success means an admin can create a complete event scale quickly, members can respond without confusion, and the interface stays usable on desktop and mobile.

## Brand Personality

Focused, clear, grounded.

The product should feel like an internal ministry operations tool: calm, practical, trustworthy, and aligned with Estacao 337. It should not feel like enterprise software selling itself, nor like a decorative landing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards with decorative analytics, ornamental gradients, inflated cards, or marketing-style hero layouts inside the app. Avoid interfaces that feel like social apps, public signup funnels, billing platforms, or multi-tenant business software. Avoid exposing local seed access details or operational credentials in documentation or UI copy.

## Design Principles

Prioritize the task over presentation: every screen should make the next operational action obvious.

Keep hierarchy calm and scannable: admins need quick comparison across members, teams, scales, and statuses.

Use standard product affordances: forms, tables, badges, side navigation, and profile menus should behave predictably.

Respect the local-first scope: do not imply backend sync, real authentication, billing, or public account management.

Design for repeated church operations: flows should be efficient for weekly scheduling, invitation response, and event preparation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, labels, focus states, keyboard navigation, and responsive behavior. Preserve React Aria semantics for form controls and interactive components. Support mobile use for members responding to scales, and avoid motion or layout patterns that interfere with task completion.
